The First NATIONAL BIOETHICS CONFERENCE
November 25, 26, and 27, 2005
YMCA International House, Mumbai Central, Mumbai

The fist National Bioethics Conference of the IJME, collaboratively organised by 20 organisations and institutions from India, will take place from November 25 to 27, 2005, in Mumbai. The conference aims to establish a regular platform for coming together, sharing experiences and fostering cooperation among individuals, organisations and institutions concerned with bioethics in India.

Conference theme

Ethical challenges in health care: global context, Indian reality

Focus sub-themes of the conference

  • Ethical challenges in HIV/AIDS
  • Ethics of life and death in the era of hi-tech health care
  • Ethical responsibilities in violence, conflict and religious strife

Conference programme

Thirteen eminent experts in bioethics will deliver keynote addresses on the main theme and focus sub-themes in five plenary sessions. Researchers, medical practitioners and activists from different parts of the country and abroad will present 64 papers in four parallel academic sessions. Ten parallel workshops have been planned during the conference. In the academic sessions as well as in the workshops, adequate time will be provided for interaction between participants and presenters. In addition, films on bioethics will be shown on the late afternoon of the first two days of the conference. The final conference programme will be available on the website from November 1, 2005 onwards. For hard copies of the programme, please contact the conference secretariat at bioethics2005@yahoo.co.in

Conference timings: November 25-26: 9 am to 9 pm, November. 27: 9.00 am. to 4 pm.
